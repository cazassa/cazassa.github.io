Fundamentals of the Market

There is strong demand for residential units regarding quantity and quality in Brazil 2013

Studies indicate quantitative deficit of 6 million units, mainly in household income under 5 minimum wages (95% of this statistics). For demographic and statistical reasons, the annual demand for real estate is around 1.5 million units per year. Meanwhile, the supply of homes has approached this figure. As a proxy, the number of housing units financed had two peaks that demonstrate how the market stood for more than 20 years in 1980 and 1990: the threshold of 600,000 units were financed in 1981 and then in 2006 again. With regard to the quality of the demand, we see a 'Europeanization' of Brazilian families (fewer people per family).

No factors back a residential or real estate bubble in Brazil

Despite the higher price levels of real estate in Brazil, a set of conditions found in real estate indicate no bubbles in asset prices in any market. In Microeconomic terms, buyers have compromised your income, creditworthiness is always assessed (and often fail potential homebuyers) and banks cannot commit patrimonially or in terms of liquidity as observed in the bubbles of assets in Spain or the USA. In Macroeconomic terms, the mortgage loans to total lending is low and demand for real estate is high. Housing deficit is greater in quantity for the lower-income public and greater in quality for higher income population. In general, the growth story is a is a long-term one. The overall credit to GDP of 40% contrasts with mortgage to GDP of about 8%.

 

The Brazilian industry of real estate funds is still very small

The size of real estate trusts or funds increased significantly between 2010 and 2013 in Brazil. The number of funds registered under CVM (the Brazilian SEC) and those traded under Bovespa total approximately 20 less value than the corresponding figure in the United States. The standard comparison of various indicators that are 10x larger in the USA, such as GDP and investments. In fact, there are still many families and individuals, if not the government or large companies, holders of real estate assets as compared to a greater democratization of ownership in most developed countries.

 

 

Did you know...

There is an informal hierarchy of profitability in the world among real estate investors sectors according

Hotels are considered riskier investments. The simple reason is the sensitivity of demand for hotels in moments of crisis. Thus, on top of profitability levels shall be the residential subsector, followed by corporate properties and shopping malls, and lastly, hotels and the like. Nevertheless, there is no doubt that a well located and well run hotel is a great investment.
